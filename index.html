ABOUT LAWRENCE GLASNER

Lawrence Glasner is one of California's leading advocates protecting the civil rights of victims of employment discrimination, sexual and racial harassment, slavery and human trafficking, whistle blowers, and of professionals and working people.

He currently provides expert consultation to non-profits on employment law, risk management, business law, and other matters. He is an experienced mediator and proven problem solver.

Mr. Glasner served in the second Reagan White House in Washington, D.C. as Executive Assistant to Herbert E. Ellingwood, Chairman of the United States Merit Systems Protection Board. He is currently a member of the Advisory Board for the Coalition Against Slavery and Human Trafficking and serves on the Board of iReachAfrica. Lawrence writes on international human rights issues, religious freedom, the death penalty, and opposing blasphemy laws. He teaches on topics including leadership, human resources, employment discrimination, civil remedies in slavery and litigating human trafficking cases.

He attended Hampshire College in Amherst, Massachusetts and completed his B.S. magna cum laude at Oral Roberts University in Tulsa, Oklahoma. He earned his M.B.A. from the University of Southern California where he was a Rockwell International Fellow, and J.D. from Pepperdine University School of Law in 1989.

Lawrence appeared as special counsel and observer at a Neo-Nazi Hate Crime trial in Cologne, Germany, addressing the German media and assisting the victim and his family.

He and his beautiful wife Tamara have been married for over thirty years. They have three adult daughters and enjoy living in Redding, California. He is an avid reader and student of the Bible. Lawrence is a musician and music lover and studied percussionwith Walter Rosenberger, formerly with the New York Philharmonic. He speaks some French, German and Japanese.
