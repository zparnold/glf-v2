ABOUT LAWRENCE GLASNER

Lawrence Glasner, J.D., MBA, is the principal attorney of The Glasner Law Firm in Redding, California.

His past activities include:

  • Political Appointee in the Reagan White House.
  • Leading civil rights and employment discrimination advocate in Southern California.
  • Successfully tried, arbitrated, or mediated over 1,100 litigated matters.
  • Advisory Board for the Coalition to Abolish Slavery and Human Trafficking.
  • Special counsel and observer at a Neo-Nazi Hate Crime trial in Cologne, Germany.
  • Speech Writer for Dr. Paul Bhatti, former Federal Minister for Religious and Minority Affairs for Pakistan.
  • Lecturer on topics ranging from employment discrimination to civil remedies in slavery and human trafficking cases.
  • Work on the death penalty published by the U.N. Commission on Human Rights.
  • Extensive writing about international human rights and refugees.
  • Extensive writing about international human rights and refugees.

Lawrence graduated magna cum laude from Oral Roberts University, earned an M.B.A. from the University of Southern California where he was a Rockwell International Fellow, and a Juris Doctorate from the Pepperdine University School of Law. He attended Christ for the Nations Institute and the Bethel School of Supernatural Ministry.

He is rated AV Preeminent by Martindale-Hubble, signifying that a lawyer’s peers rank him at the highest level of professional excellence and ethics. Los Angeles Magazine named him a Top Lawyer in recognition for excellence and achievements by the legal community. He was recently designated a Northern California Super Lawyer placing him in the top 5% of attorneys in his field.

Lawrence currently serves on the Board of Directors of I Reach Africa.

He and his wife, Tamara, have been happily married since 1983 and have three lovely daughters whom they love spending time. He studied percussion with the late Walter Rosenberger of the N.Y. Philharmonic, is an audiophile, music lover (Bach to Blue Note) and plays piano, guitar, and drums. Lawrence actively mentors young professionals and writers. Chuck Norris once punched him in the sternum during a karate class in the 70’s, and he continues to struggle to apprehend French German and Japanese.
